Carrot Soup with Tangled Collard Greens and Dukkah

Ingredients

  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 1/4 lb carrots, scrubbed and thinly sliced
  • 1 tbsp coconut oil
  • 1 bunch collard greens
  • 1/4 cup coriander seeds
  • 1/4 cup cumin seeds
  • 3 pinches of dried thyme
  • 1 tbsp grated ginger
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • juice of 1/2 lime
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 pinches of dried oregano
  • 1 cup pistachios
  • sea salt
  • 1/2 cup sesame seeds

Preparation

  • Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. add the onion, carrots, ginger, cumin and 1 tsp salt. Cook, stirring frequently, about 6 minutes.
  • Add 4 cups of water and bring to a boil. Lower to a simmer, and cook, covered for about 20 minutes, or until vegetables are tender.While the soup is cooking, remove the collard leaves from the stems.
  • Roll them up and then cut them into thin strips.
  • Put the strips in a second pot with 1/2 cup water and a pinch of salt. Steam until tender, about 4-5 minutes.
  • Drain and toss with the coconut butter and lime juice. Season with salt to taste.When the soup is ready, puree it until smooth. Season to taste with salt.
  • Serve topped with collard greens and a sprinkle of Dukkah.
  • Heat the oven to 35
  • Spread the pistachios, sesame seeds, coriander seeds, and cumin seeds in a baking pan. Toast until fragrant, about 8 minutes.
  • Let cool for a few minutes.
  • Transfer the nuts and seeds, thyme oregano, and 3/4 tsp salt to a food processor. Pulse until roughly ground but still crunchy. Season with pepper.
