Cucumber Noodles with Asparagus and Ginger Scallion Sesame Sauce (Gluten-Free, Grain-Free, Vegan, Paleo)

Ingredients

  • 1 bunch asparagus, ends trimmed and sliced on the diagonal into 2 inch pieces
  • 1 1/2 Tablespoons/23ml coconut aminos or gluten-free tamari
  • 2 English hothouse cucumbers, peeled
  • 1 2 to 3 inch piece of fresh ginger, peeled and grated on a microplane or fine zester
  • 2 Tablespoons/30ml olive oil (chose one with a fairly light and neutral flavor)
  • 1 /8th teaspoon red pepper flakes , or more to taste
  • 2 to 3 small scallions (green onions), thinly sliced
  • Pinch of sea salt, to taste
  • 1 teaspoon/5ml toasted sesame oil
  • 2 Tablespoons/30g toasted sesame seeds to garnish (optional)

Preparation

  • In a large bowl whisk together the scallions, ginger, toasted sesame oil , olive oil, coconut aminos , red pepper flakes . Taste and add a pinch of salt if needed. Set aside while you make the asparagus and cucumber noodles. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Blanch the asparagus spears until crisp tender (about 2 minutes) then drain and shock in a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking and set color.Using a julienne peeler or spiralizer create long noodles from the peeled cucumbers. (If using a julienne peeler, run it lengthwise until you reach the core then turn a ¼ turn and repeat all the way around. Toss the cucumber noodles with the ginger scallion sauce, add the cooled blanched asparagus and toss gently again to combine.
  • Sprinkle with toasted sesame seeds and serve.
