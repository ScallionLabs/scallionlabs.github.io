Spicy Three-Bean and Corn Chili

Ingredients

  • 1 tbsp ancho chili powder
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 28 oz canned diced tomatoes
  • 4 oz minced mild green chiles
  • 2 tsp cocoa powder
  • 1 (15 oz) can corn kernels, drained
  • 2/3 cup dried black beans, soaked overnight
  • 2/3 cup dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 2/3 cup dried pinto beans, soaked overnight
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 onion, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • salt, to taste
  • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar
  • 1 tbsp smoked paprika
  • 1 medium sweet potato, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 1/2 tsp freshly ground white pepper

Preparation

  • If using dried beans, put in a large pot and cover with salted water. Bring to a boil, then cover and simmer until tender, about an hour.
  • Remove from the heat and drain, reserving the cooking liquid.In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  • Add in the onion, sweet potato and garlic. Cook over medium heat, stirring occasionally for 8 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
  • Add the drained beans, corn, chiles, and tomato to the pot. Stir to combine.
  • Add in 4 cups of the bean liquid and bring to a simmer. Stir in the vinegar, brown sugar, chili powder, smoked paprika, and white pepper. Simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally, or until the mixture thickens a bit. Season to taste.
  • Serve topped with cheese or sour cream.
