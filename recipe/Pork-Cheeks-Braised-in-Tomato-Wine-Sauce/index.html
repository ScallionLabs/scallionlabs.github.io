Pork Cheeks Braised in Tomato Wine Sauce

Ingredients

  • 1 cup chopped canned tomatoes
  • A few tablespoons oil, for browning
  • 2 medium carrots, peeled, cut into 1/2 inch rounds
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped into 1/4-inch dice
  • 2 cups dry red wine
  • 1 tablespoon chopped fresh herbs, such as rosemary or thyme
  • 1 medium onion, chopped into 1/4-inch dice
  • 2.5 to 3 pounds pork cheeks

Preparation

  • In the skillet, heat the oil over medium heat. Make sure the pork cheeks are dry and sprinkle them with salt and pepper.
  • Add them to the pan, taking care not to crowd the meat. Cook until the surface is browned on both sides, about 4 minutes per side, flipping only once or twice so as not to disturb browning.
  • Remove with tongs as cheeks are browned; set aside.
  • In the meantime, preheat oven to 300°F.
  • Add onions, carrots, and celery to pot. Sauté until softened but not brown, about 5 minutes.
  • Add wine, tomato, and rosemary. Bring to a simmer.
  • Add the pork cheeks and stir to coat the meat in the sauce.
  • Cover the pan and transfer to the oven. Braise until meat is very tender, about 2.5 to 3 hours.
  • Serve with pasta or polenta.
