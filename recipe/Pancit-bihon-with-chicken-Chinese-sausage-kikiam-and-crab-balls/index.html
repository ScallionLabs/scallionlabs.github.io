Ingredients

  • about 1 c. of shredded cabbage
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 2 celery stalks, thinly sliced
  • 4 cloves of garlic, mined
  • ground black pepper, to taste
  • about 1 c. of sliced seafood balls (whatever combination you prefer)
  • 1 onion, thinly sliced
  • a generous pinch of salt
  • 1 Chinese sausage, thinly sliced
  • soy sauce, to taste
  • 2 tbsps. of vegetable cooking oil
  • about 80 g. of dried bihon, soaked in cold water for about 20 minutes then drained
  • about 1/2 c. of flaked or chopped cooked chicken (pork is good too)
  • about 10 pcs. of chicharo, trimmed and stringed, and cut into halves if large

  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 2 celery stalks, thinly sliced

  • 1 Chinese sausage, thinly sliced

Preparation

  • Heat the cooking oil in a wok or frying pan.
  • Add the sliced seafood balls and cook, over high heat, just until the edges start to brown.
  • Add the slices of Chinese sausage and the chicken. Cook, still over high heat, until the sausage slices change color and lose their raw appearance (pay attention to the texture of the fat in the sausage — that’s how you can tell if the sausage is sufficiently cooked).
  • Add the garlic and onion. Stir fry for about 30 seconds then add the rest of the vegetables.
  • Sprinkle in the salt and some pepper. Don’t be tempted to season with soy sauce at this point because the vegetables will look too dark by the time the dish is done. Plus, they won’t benefit from the contact with too much liquid. Reserve the soy sauce for flavoring and coloring the noodles. So, season the vegetables with salt and continue stir frying for another minute.
  • Add the drained noodles.
  • Pour in about 2 tbsps. of soy sauce and more pepper. Toss and stir alternately until the noodles are heated through and the coloring looks uniform.Taste, add more soy sauce and pepper, if needed.
  • Serve with kalamansi halves (or lemon slices) on the side.
