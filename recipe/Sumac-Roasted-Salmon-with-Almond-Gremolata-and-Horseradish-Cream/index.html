Sumac Roasted Salmon with Almond Gremolata and Horseradish Cream

Ingredients

  • ¼ cup salted almonds, chopped (you can sub in another nut like walnuts)
  • ¼ cup chives, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 1 medium garlic clove, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ground sumac
  • 1/3 cup prepared horseradish, drained/dried very well
  • ½ teaspoon kosher salt
  • Kosher salt and pepper to taste
  • Lemon juice to finish
  • 2 teaspoons lemon juice
  • ½ tablespoon lemon zest (about 1 medium lemon)
  • ¼ cup parsley, chopped
  • ¼ teaspoon fresh cracked black pepper
  • 1 pound salmon fillet
  • 1 cup sour cream (can be dairy free)

Preparation

  • To make horseradish sour cream, whisk together sour cream, horseradish, lemon juice and chives. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Refrigerate until ready to serve. Can be made 3 days ahead of time.Preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  • Place salmon on a foil lined baking sheet and rub with 1 tablespoon olive oil. Season with salt, pepper and sumac.Roast salmon for 12-15 minutes or until desired doneness. Finish with a squeeze of lemon juice and a drizzle of olive oil.While the salmon is roasting, combine the gremolata ingredients together in a small bowl. Store leftovers in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.]
  • Serve salmon with horseradish sour cream and parsley almond gremolata
