Baked Tilapia with Quinoa and Garlicky Green Beans

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon extra virgin olive oil
  • 12 ounces fresh green beans, cleaned
  • 1 tablespoon fresh minced garlic
  • Juice from ½ fresh lemon
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • Zest from one lemon
  • ½ teaspoon dry marjoram
  • 4 ounces total of any combination of sliced red onion, red bell pepper, orange bell pepper and yellow bell pepper
  • Few grinds black pepper
  • 1 cup quinoa
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tablespoons minced shallots
  • 4 tilapia filets (about one pound)
  • 2 cups vegetable stock (or 2 cups water)
  • ¼ cup white wine
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme or ½ teaspoon dry

Preparation

  • Preheat oven to 425 degrees.In a small sauté pan, over medium heat sauté shallots in olive oil until just starting to get soft (about 2 to 3 minutes).
  • Add thyme, lemon zest, salt and pepper, stir and cook for one minute.
  • Lay tilapia flat in a small baking pan and brush with lemon juice. Then spread shallot mixture over each filet. Set aside.In the small sauté pan, add all ingredients for vegetable topping but do not cook yet.In a medium sauté pan, over medium heat, place olive oil and garlic and cook for two minutes.
  • Remove from heat and add marjoram, green beans, salt and pepper and set aside. (Hold off on finishing cooking the beans – but have the wine and lemon juice ready for the final steps in this recipe.)In a small sauce pan, place stock, quinoa, thyme sprigs and pepper. Cover and bring to a boil. Lower heat to a low simmer and cook 12 minutes.
  • Remove from heat but leave covered and let sit for 15 minutes untouched.At the same time you start the quinoa, place tilapia in the oven, uncovered. The tilapia should take between 15 and 25 minutes to cook depending how thick the filets are.When there is ten minutes left for the quinoa, heat the two pans (vegetable topping and green beans) over medium heat. Cook both just until tender but still crisp.
  • Remove topping from heat and set aside.
  • Add wine to beans, raise heat and cook for a few minutes more.
  • Remove from heat and squeeze the tablespoon of lemon juice over the green beans. Before serving, remove and discard thyme sprig from quinoa. To serve, place a tilapia portion, ¼ of the quinoa, ¼ of the beans and ¼ of the sauté vegetables onto a dinner plate for each guest.
