Grilled Fish Tacos

Macros

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon hot sauce or ¼ teaspoon ground chipotle powder (optional) (taco on the left has sauce with chipotle powder, taco on the right has sauce without)
  • 1 pound firm white fish, such as halibut or mahi mahi
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • ½ teaspoon ground cumin
  • ¼ cup freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 1 Tablespoon freshly squeezed lime juice
  • 6 Tablespoons mayonnaise (I like soy-free Vegenaise)
  • Oil (for brushing grill)
  • 2 Tablespoons unrefined olive oil
  • 8 6-inch corn tortillas, warmed either on grill or heated griddle pan until pliable
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • fresh pico de gallo (salsa), fresh lime cut into wedges
  • Sliced avocado, guacamole, pickled red onion or radishes, shredded napa, savoy or green cabbage or slaw,
  • Dry Rub
  • ½ teaspoon sea salt
  • pinch of sea salt
  • sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 6 Tablespoons whole unsweetened yogurt
  • OR

Preparation

  • Whisk together ingredients for white sauce and refrigerate until ready to use.
  • Combine lime juice, oil and cumin in a shallow nonreactive dish small enough to just hold the fish.
  • Add fish and turn to coat in the marinade. Season with salt and pepper and marinate fish at room temperature for 30 minutes. OR mix dry rub spices together and sprinkle on both sides of fish.Preheat grill to medium-hot.
  • Brush grill grate with oil. Grill fish until just cooked through, about 3-5 minutes per side. Break fish into large pieces.Fill tortilla with fish and desired accompaniments.
  • Drizzle with white sauce and squeeze fresh lime wedges over tacos.
