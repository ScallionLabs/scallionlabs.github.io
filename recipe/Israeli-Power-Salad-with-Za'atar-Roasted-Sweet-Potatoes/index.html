Israeli Power Salad with Za’atar Roasted Sweet Potatoes

  • https://www.hummusapien.com/israeli-power-salad/
  • Servings: 4
  • Ready in: 50min
  • Preparation time: 15min
  • Cooking time: 35min
  • Tags:

Ingredients

  • 1-15oz can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 bunch chard, de-stemmed and sliced into ribbons (about 3 cups)
  • ½ tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • ½ cup flat leaf parsley, chopped
  • ½ cup sliced green onions
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice
  • Lots of freshly ground black pepper
  • 3 Persian or mini seedless cucumbers, diced (1½ cups)
  • 2 cups thinly sliced red cabbage (Brussels sprouts are also good!_
  • ½ tsp sea salt, to taste
  • 1 large sweet potato, diced
  • 1/3 cup tahini
  • Heaping ½ cup walnuts, chopped and toasted
  • ½ tbsp za'atar
  • 1 tbsp za'atar, to taste

Preparation

  • Preheat oven to 400F.
  • Place sweet potatoes on a lined baking sheet.
  • Drizzle with oil and sprinkle with za'atar and a good pinch of salt and pepper, tossing to combine.
  • Roast for 30-35 minutes, or until fork tender.
  • Place walnuts on a small baking sheet or piece of foil. Toast on the rack below sweet potatoes for 5-7 minutes, or until golden and fragrant.
  • Remove and set aside while sweet potatoes continue to cook.
  • Meanwhile, chop all your veggies.
  • Add chard, green onions, cabbage, cucumbers, parsley, walnuts, and chickpeas to a large mixing bowl, stirring to combine.
  • Add tahini, lemon juice, za'atar, salt, and lots of pepper. Stir to combine and season to taste.
  • Let sweet potatoes cool slightly before stirring into salad and serving. Leftovers keep well in an air-tight container in the fridge for a couple days. Great for packing lunches!
