Mushroom Spinach & Gruyere Crustless Quiche

  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 6-oz. bag of baby spinach
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 cup shredded Gruyere cheese
  • 1 cup half-and-half
  • 2 cups chopped mushrooms (1 8-oz. package of mushrooms yields about this much coarsely chopped)

Preparation

  • Preheat oven to 375 degrees. Butter a 9-inch pie plate and set aside.
  • Place the spinach in a large skillet, cover, and cook over medium heat until wilted, about 3 minutes.
  • Remove from the heat and spoon into a strainer set over the sink or a bowl. Allow to cool.
  • Using the back of a spoon, press on the spinach to remove most of the moisture, then squeeze it in your hands to remove as much liquid as possible. Move to a cutting board and coarsely chop.
  • Place between two layers of paper towels and press to remove moisture. Set aside.
  • Using the same skillet that you used for the spinach, heat the olive oil over medium heat.
  • Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring until tender, about 5 minutes.
  • Add the garlic and cook for 1 additional minute.
  • Add the spinach and cook, stirring, until any remaining moisture has evaporated, about 2 minutes.
  • Remove from heat.
  • Add a pinch of salt and pepper to taste.
  • In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs until light. Stir in the half and half. Spoon the spinach mixture evenly into buttered pie dish.
  • Sprinkle with the cheese.
  • Pour in the egg mixture.
  • Bake the quiche until puffed and golden brown, about 35 minutes.
  • Serve warm or at room temperature.
