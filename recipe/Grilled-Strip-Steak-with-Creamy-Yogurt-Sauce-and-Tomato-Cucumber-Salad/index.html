Preparation

  • Split each steak in half crosswise to form four 8-ounce steaks. Carefully press dry with paper towels and set aside on a plate.
  • Combine tomatoes and cucumbers in a colander and season with salt and pepper. Set in sink or bowl to drain while you prepare the yogurt sauce.
  • Finely chop 3/4 of the parsley and reserve remaining leaves.
  • Combine chopped parsley, yogurt, 2 teaspoons lemon juice, and 2 tablespoons olive oil in a bowl. Stir to combine and season to taste with salt and pepper.
  • Light one chimney full of charcoal. When all the charcoal is lit and covered with gray ash, pour out and spread the coals evenly over half of coal grate. Alternatively, set half the burners of a gas grill to high heat. Set cooking grate in place, cover gill and allow to preheat for 5 minutes. Clean and oil the grilling grate.   • While steaks are resting, toss tomatoes, cucumbers, and remaining parsley leaves with remaining teaspoon lemon juice and tablespoon olive oil.
  • Slice steak, sprinkle with coarse sea salt, and serve immediately with yogurt sauce and cucumber-tomato salad.
  • Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil just before serving.
