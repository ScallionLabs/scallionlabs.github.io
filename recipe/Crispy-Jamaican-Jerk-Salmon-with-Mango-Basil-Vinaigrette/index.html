Crispy Jamaican Jerk Salmon with Mango-Basil Vinaigrette

  • http://www.theroastedroot.net/crispy-jamaican-jerk-salmon-with-mango-basil-vinaigrette/
  • Servings: 3
  • Ready in: 30min
  • Preparation time: 20min
  • Cooking time: 10min

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon Jamaican Jerk spice (see note)*
  • 1 ripe but firm avocado, peeled and sliced
  • ½ cup fresh blueberries
  • ½ cup fresh basil, chopped
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, peeled and grated
  • ½ cup full-fat canned coconut milk
  • 5 ounces field greens, chopped
  • 1 large ripe mango, peeled and chopped
  • 2 teaspoons pure maple syrup
  • ¼ cup olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon grapeseed or olive oil
  • ½ red bell pepper, cut into matchsticks
  • ¼ red onion, thinly sliced
  • ¼ cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 pound salmon fillet
  • ¼ teaspoon sea salt, to taste

Preparation

  • Add all ingredients for the vinaigrette except for the basil to a high-powered blender (I used my Magic Bullet). Blend until completely smooth.
  • Transfer to a jar and stir in the chopped basil. Refrigerate until ready to use.
  • Add all ingredients for the salad to a large serving bowl and toss together.
  • Place your oven on the high broil setting and move one of the racks to the top shelf.
  • Place salmon fillet in a large casserole dish and drizzle with oil. Use your hands to be sure the whole fillet is evenly coated.
  • Sprinkle the Jamaican jerk spice over the salmon fillet and very gently rub into the salmon to ensure the whole fillet is evenly coated in oil and spice.
  • Place on the top shelf under the preheated broiler and broil for 10 to 15 minutes (depending on size of fillet. A fillet less than 1-inch thick should only take 10 to 12 minutes). Note: Keep the oven light on to ensure the salmon does not catch fire - while this has never happened to me, it is possible for food to ignite when its under the broiler.
  • Remove salmon from oven and serve with salad and mango-basil vinaigrette.
