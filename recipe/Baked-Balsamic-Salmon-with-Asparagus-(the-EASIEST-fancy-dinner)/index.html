Baked Balsamic Salmon with Asparagus (the EASIEST fancy dinner)

Ingredients

  • 1 lb. asparagus, ends trimmed
  • 11 oz. bag fresh baby spinach
  • Balsamic glaze
  • fresh basil pesto, preferably homemade*
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • kosher salt and black pepper
  • fresh lemon, optional
  • olive oil
  • 4 (5-7 oz. each) salmon fillets, rinsed and pat dried
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp. unsalted butter
  • 1 lb. asparagus, ends trimmed
  • 11 oz. bag fresh baby spinach
  • Balsamic glaze
  • fresh basil pesto, preferably homemade*
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • fresh lemon, optional
  • 4 (5-7 oz. each) salmon fillets, rinsed and pat dried
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 2 Tbsp. unsalted butter

Preparation

  • Preheat the oven to 425 degrees F. Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper.
  • Add salmon to one baking sheet. Rub with little olive oil and season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  • Add asparagus to the second baking sheet and toss with little olive oil. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
  • Place salmon on the middle rack and asparagus on the lower rack and bake for 12 - 15 minutes. When done, salmon should easily flake and asparagus be tender but crisp.
  • Meanwhile, heat about 1-2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large saut pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add garlic and just when it becomes fragrant, immediately add spinach, tossing constantly with tongs until wilted. If it begins to stick to the pan, add water by 1 Tablespoon.
  • Remove from heat. Stir in butter and season with salt and pepper.
  • To assemble: divide the spinach, salmon, and asparagus among four plates. Top with chopped tomato. Spoon lightly some of the basil pesto and add a drizzle of balsamic glaze. If desired, add a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
  • Serve immediately.
