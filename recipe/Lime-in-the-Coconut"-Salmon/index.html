Ingredients

  • 1/8 teaspoon cayenne pepper or black pepper
  • the zest of 2 limes plus 3 tablespoons fresh lime juice, plus lime wedges for serving
  • 2 pounds wild salmon (I used 2 pieces that were 1 pound each)
  • 1 cup unsweetened coconut flakes
  • 1 can unsweetened coconut milk

Preparation

  • In a shallow pan whisk the coconut milk and lime juice together.
  • Place the salmon skin side up into the pan, cover and refrgerate at least 1 hour or longer.
  • Heat a frying pan over medium heat and toast the cashews, stirring often, until they get some lightly golden spots on them.
  • Remove from heat and cool.Pulse the cashews in a food processor until finely ground.
  • Add the coconut, salt, cayenne and lime zest; pulse until combined. Preheat oven to 400 degrees F.
  • Remove the salmon from the coconut milk and place skin side down in a large roasting pan or baking sheet.Spoon the coconut cashew mixture evenly over them and press it down with your hands.
  • Bake the salmon until cooked through 20 - 25 minutes, depending on their size - internal temperature on a meat thermometer should reach 140 - 145 degrees F.
  • Serve with lime wedges, lemon tarter sauce and/or blueberry corn salsa.
