Grilled Rib-eye with Tomato Salad & Chimichurri Sauce

Ingredients

  • 1/4 teaspoon ground chipotle pepper or cayenne pepper
  • 1 cup packed flat-leaf parsley leaves (from 1 large bunch)
  • 1 small clove garlic, chopped
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon extra-virgin olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground pepper
  • 1 pound boneless rib-eye steak, about 1 inch thick, trimmed of fat and cut into 4 servings
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced sweet onion
  • 4 medium tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 tablespoon distilled white vinegar
  • 3 tablespoons distilled white vinegar

Preparation

  • To prepare the sauce: Chop parsley and garlic together on a cutting board until the parsley is finely minced.
Transfer to a medium bowl, add vinegar, oil, 1/2 teaspoon salt and chipotle (or cayenne) pepper; stir to combine.Preheat grill to high.To prepare the salad
  • Combine tomatoes, onion, oil and vinegar in a medium bowl. Season with salt and pepper.To prepare the steak: Rub steak with oil. Season on both sides with salt and pepper. Grill the steak 3 to 4 minutes per side for medium-rare. Allow the steak to rest 5 minutes; serve with the salad on the side and a dollop of the Chimichurri Sauce on top.
