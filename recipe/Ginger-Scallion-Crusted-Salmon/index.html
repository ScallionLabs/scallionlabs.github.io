Ginger-Scallion Crusted Salmon

Ingredients

  • 1 teaspoon black sesame seeds
  • 2 cucumbers, halved, seeded, and cut into very thin slices
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill
  • 1/3 cup minced fresh ginger root
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 green onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
  • Kosher salt to taste
  • 1/4 cup mirin (Japanese rice wine)
  • 1/4 cup peanut oil
  • 2 tablespoons peanut oil
  • 3 tablespoons ponzu sauce
  • 1/2 cup rice wine vinegar
  • 4 (8 ounce) center-cut salmon fillets
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 shallots, minced
  • 1/2 cup cold unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1 teaspoon white sesame seeds

Preparation

  • To make the cucumber salad, stir together sesame oil, dill, rice vinegar, mirin, and sesame seeds in a glass or plastic bowl; add cucumber, and toss until well coated. Cover, and refrigerate for 2 hours.
  • Let sit at room temperature for 30 minutes before serving.
  • To prepare the ginger crust, stir together the ginger, green onions, and garlic in a sturdy, metal bowl; set aside.
  • Heat 1/4 cup peanut oil in a small saucepan over high heat until it begins to smoke.
  • Remove from heat, and carefully stir in the ginger mixture; set aside.
  • Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C).
  • Heat 2 tablespoons peanut oil in a large, ovenproof, nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Season salmon fillets to taste with salt and pepper. Sear salmon until golden brown on both sides, 30 to 40 seconds per side.
  • Transfer to a plate, and wipe excess oil from skillet.
  • Spread honey over top of fillets, then evenly spread with reserved ginger crust.
  • Replace salmon into the skillet, and place into the oven.
  • Bake until the center of the salmon turns opaque, 6 to 7 minutes.
  • Meanwhile, melt 2 tablespoons butter in a small saucepan over medium heat, stir in shallots, and cook until softened and translucent, about 2 minutes.
  • Pour in white wine, then simmer until nearly evaporated.
  • Add the ponzu sauce and heavy cream, reduce by 1/
  • Remove from heat, then whisk in cold butter pieces until dissolved; season to taste with kosher salt.
  • To assemble the dish, season the cucumber salad to taste with salt. Divide the salad evenly onto four plates, spreading it out into a 4 to 5-inch circle.
  • Drizzle the ponzu butter onto the bare area of the plate, around the cucumber salad.
  • Place a salmon fillet, crust-side up, on top of the salad, and serve.
