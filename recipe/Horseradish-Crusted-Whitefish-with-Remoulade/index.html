Horseradish-Crusted Whitefish with Rémoulade

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/4 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 2 tablespoons minced garlic
  • 1/3 cup grainy mustard
  • 2 tablespoons grainy mustard
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons drained bottled horseradish
  • 2 tablespoons drained bottled horseradish
  • 1/4 teaspoon hot sauce such as Tabasco
  • 1/4 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon zest
  • 1 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/3 cup plus 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 cup panko (Japanese bread crumbs)
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
  • 1/4 cup chopped scallion
  • 4 (8-oz) whitefish fillets (about 3/4 inch thick), pin bones removed
  • 1/4 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce

Preparation

Make rmoulade
  • Pulse all rmoulade ingredients in a food processor until combined, then transfer to a bowl.
Make fish
  • Preheat oven to 350F with rack in middle.
  • Stir together parsley, horseradish, mustard, garlic, and zest. Gradually stir in panko, then drizzle with 1/3 cup olive oil and gently stir.
  • Pat fish dry and arrange, skin side down, on a large platter.
  • Brush flesh with 1 tablespoon oil, then season with 1/2 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon pepper. Gently pat about 1/2 cup panko mixture evenly onto each fillet.
  • Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a 12-inch nonstick skillet over medium-high heat until hot, then saut fillets in 2 batches, crumb side down first, carefully turning once with a large spatula, until golden on both sides, about 6 minutes total per batch.
  • Transfer to a large shallow baking pan. (Fish will not be fully cooked.) Wipe skillet clean and add remaining tablespoon oil for second batch.
  • Bake fish in oven until just cooked through, about 5 minutes.
  • Serve with rmoulade.
  • Cooks' note:Rmoulade can be made 1 day ahead and chilled, covered. Bring to room temperature before serving.
